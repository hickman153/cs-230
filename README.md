# CS-230 Operating Platforms

## Portfolio Reflection: Draw It or Lose It Software Design Document

### Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements.
The Gaming Room wanted to expand its Draw It or Lose It game into a distributed web-based application capable of supporting multiple teams, players, and concurrent users. The client required a scalable and reliable software solution that could operate across multiple platforms while maintaining centralized game management and data consistency. The software design focused on supporting future growth, cross-platform compatibility, and long-term maintainability.

### What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
I believe I did particularly well in evaluating multiple operating platforms and connecting technical recommendations to the client's business requirements. Rather than simply selecting technologies, I explained how factors such as scalability, security, maintainability, and platform compatibility influenced the design decisions. The recommendations section provided clear justification for selecting a Linux-based server environment and a distributed architecture.

### What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
Working through the design document helped me think about system requirements before implementation. Considering architecture, security, scalability, storage, and platform compatibility early in the process helped establish a clear direction for development. The design document also encouraged me to evaluate alternative solutions and understand the impact of technical decisions before writing code.

### If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I were revising the document, I would expand the system architecture section. While the document describes the distributed client-server model, I would include more detail regarding component interactions, deployment considerations, and communication between services. Additional diagrams and architectural details would improve clarity and provide a more complete picture of how the system would operate in a production environment.

### How did you interpret the user's needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user's needs when designing?
I interpreted the client's needs by identifying the primary goals of scalability, reliability, centralized game management, and cross-platform compatibility. These requirements influenced the recommendation of a distributed architecture, centralized game services, and a Linux-based server environment. Considering user and client needs is important because the success of a software solution depends on how effectively it addresses the problems the client is trying to solve. A technically sound design that does not meet user requirements cannot be considered successful.

### How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
My approach focused on understanding the requirements before evaluating technical solutions. I analyzed the client's objectives, identified system constraints, and compared different platform and architecture options before making recommendations. In future projects, I would continue using requirements analysis, architectural evaluation, and object-oriented design principles to guide development. I would also spend additional time creating detailed architectural diagrams and evaluating scalability, security, and maintainability requirements early in the design process.
